the story

Witcher 3

The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt (Polish: Wiedźmin 3: Dziki Gon, English: The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt) is a computer game in the action/RPG genre, developed and published by the Polish studio CD Projekt RED. The game was originally released on May 19, 2015 on Windows, PlayStation 4 and Xbox One, then on October 15, 2019 on Nintendo Switch, and on December 14, 2022 on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X/S. It is a sequel to The Witcher (2007) and The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings (2011). This is the third game set in the literary universe of the Witcher book series created by Polish writer Andrzej Sapkowski, and the last to follow the adventures of Geralt of Rivia. The fantasy game, whose world is based on Slavic mythology, tells the story of the witcher Geralt of Rivia, a monster hunter whose adopted daughter Ciri is in danger, being pursued by the Wild Hunt - a mysterious otherworldly force whose secret is revealed as the game progresses. Skip to section "#Plot" Many details of the plot refer to the books written by Sapkowski, but the plot remains connected to the first two parts and brings to a conclusion the trilogy begun by the first game, released eight years earlier. Unlike previous games, The Witcher 3 takes place in an open world and focuses on using Geralt's combat and detective skills to complete orders and explore the environment. All of this is part of a multi-level main quest with many side missions, which can be completed with 36 different endings. Skip to the "#Gameplay" section The game was developed over three and a half years on a budget close to the budgets of AAA games of large Western studios.

Main cities

Novigrad

Novigrad (orig. Novigrad) is a city-state and the largest human settlement in the Northern Kingdoms, located in the delta of the Pontar River in the territory of the kingdom of Redania. It has the status of a “Free City” and is a vassal of the Redanian crown, in connection with which the hierarch faces a number of obligations spelled out in the act of founding the Free City of Novigrad in 1065[1]. Being the largest port of the Continent, the city is rightly called the “capital of the world” and the center of culture. The population of the city, according to rough estimates, is 30,000 inhabitants, of which about 6,000 are non-humans[1]. The cult of the Eternal Flame is widespread in the city. Power is represented by the hierarch Hemmelfart.

There is no Destination... It does not exist. The only thing that is destined for everyone is death.

— Geralt of Rivia

Geralt of Rivia, nicknamed the White Wolf and the Butcher of Blaviken (orig. Geralt z Rivii) is the main character of the literary saga and the protagonist of subsequent games, a witcher from the School of the Wolf, a professional monster hunter, one of the best swordsmen in the North. Has a romantic interest in two sorceresses: Yennefer of Vengerberg and Triss Merigold. His best friends, besides the witchers from his school, are a bard named Dandelion, the dwarf Zoltan Chivay and the high vampire Emiel Regis.
